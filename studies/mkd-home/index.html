mkd Home

A Study of Stillness
Setting

Home is often the first place where a way of looking is formed. Not through anything dramatic, but through repetition. The same table in different light. The same garden seen through glass. A lamp switched on before evening. A cup left beside a machine. Small arrangements that become familiar enough to disappear, until attention brings them back into view.

mkd Home is a study of that closeness. A place observed not as a project or destination, but as the quiet ground beneath the work.

Ritual

The photographs return to the ordinary things that give a day its shape. Coffee made slowly. Light gathering on a surface. Flowers kept in a corner. A room waiting before anyone enters it. Nothing is staged to become important, but each detail begins to hold meaning through use.

This is where the language of the studio begins: in the spaces between movement, in the objects that stay behind, and in the atmosphere that gathers around them.

Stillness

The home is not presented as an interior. It is treated as a place with its own rhythm. The garden presses against the windows. Warm light sits inside the rooms. Shadows collect around edges, corners and mirrors. What matters is not decoration, but feeling.

The images look for the point where a room becomes more than a room. Where familiarity turns into memory, and where the ordinary starts to carry its own quiet weight.

Response

mkd Home belongs in the archive because it explains something essential about the work. The same attention given to hotels, cities, cafés and experiences begins here, with the small act of noticing what is already close.

It is a study of home as atmosphere, but also of home as origin. A reminder that place is not defined by scale. Sometimes it is held in a lamp, a table, a garden, a flower, or the light left behind at the end of a day.
