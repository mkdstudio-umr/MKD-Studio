Places

What a city reveals when nothing particular is happening.

A city gives you its official version first. The monument, the view, the itinerary arranged by someone who has never been there in February. These studies were made after that version runs out, in the mornings, in the hours when ordinary life continues, in the light that falls across stone at an angle nobody planned for. Cities and coastlines. Medinas and gardens. The feeling of a place before it resolves into familiarity.

The camera can be used to confirm what a city already looks like or to look for what it actually is. The difference is in what you photograph and when. At the Nyhavn canal in Copenhagen, thousands of photographs are made every day, nearly all of them showing the same thing. The study there was made in the early morning, when the canal holds a different quality of light and the colour of the water is not the colour anyone expects.

These studies cover four continents without any unified geography beyond curiosity. The medinas of Tangier and Marrakech. The stone city of Porto, the light in Madrid. Singapore and Kuala Lumpur. The French Alps in winter. Corfu in the hour after arrival, when the rooms still feel like someone else's. The question in each case is the same: what does this place feel like before it becomes familiar? The photographs do not explain. They look.

A study made anywhere is a study of attention. Not the place itself, which goes on regardless, but the quality of looking that a place makes possible.
