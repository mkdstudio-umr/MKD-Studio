Al Mourjan Lounge

Between departures
بين الرحلات
Setting

Water first. A pool the length of a room, holding the ceiling on its surface. A staircase curves above it, in no particular hurry to arrive anywhere.

Read in isolation, this could be a hotel, a gallery, a members’ club. It is none of these.

Stillness

A place built for waiting, inside a building built for movement.

Beyond the walls, everything is in motion. Gates, screens, departures, the particular low-grade anxiety of air travel. Here, the brief is the opposite: slow the hour down. The lounge succeeds because it does not try to distract from the fact that you are still at an airport. It simply makes that fact irrelevant for the duration of a meal.

Material

The materials hold the room: dark water, timber, leather, polished steel catching the light. The frames follow the way the room asks you to move. Slowly, without pressure to arrive anywhere in particular.

Only later does the context arrive. The hall. The signs. The yellow bear beneath its lamp. The reminder that all of this stillness sits a few minutes from the gates.

Response

Departure is the only thing on anyone’s mind. The room is built to let you forget it, and it does so entirely through architecture and atmosphere rather than distraction. There is no screen showing departure times inside the lounge itself. You leave when you choose to, not when you are reminded to.
