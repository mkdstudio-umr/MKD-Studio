The long form.

Where a study is a chapter, a publication is the book. These are complete editorial volumes, built for a single place, brand or world, and read as a sequence rather than a page.

What mkd Publications Are

mkd STUDIO publishes selected work as long-form volumes when a story earns more than a single study can hold. A publication runs to a hundred pages or more, designed as a digital reader with its own pacing, structure and typography, built specifically for the place or world it documents. Each volume is also produced as a physical book, a printed first edition.

A single photograph can describe a moment. A publication has room to describe something larger. It can hold atmosphere, sequence, rhythm and context in a way that a gallery or a feed cannot. The subject is given the space it deserves, and the reader is given something worth returning to.

The same approach can be commissioned for a hotel, a brand or a house marking a season, an opening or a moment worth keeping. The archive publications are made independently. The commissioned volumes are built to the same standard, for a specific audience, with a specific purpose.

Publication 001

The Spaces Between

Formula One, Volume I

Ten chapters, one weekend, Silverstone and Qatar. Most people watch the race. This volume watches everything else: the hour before the gates open, the fence that separates witnessed from unwitnessed, the rain, the red flag, the five lights that mean everything and nothing until they go dark. A record of a Grand Prix from the spaces the cameras don’t choose.

Publication 002

The Ned, Doha

Place / Volume I

Scale, held quietly. The Ned Doha occupies a building that could overwhelm, and chooses not to. This volume follows that restraint through marble corridors, members’ rooms and the spaces between arrival and rest, where luxury is measured in the absence of effort rather than its display.

Publication 003

Waiting for Dusk

Experience / Volume I

Ten photographs made at Doha Golf Club during the Commercial Bank Qatar Masters. The sport is the reason everyone is there. This volume attends to the rest: the caddies crossing the fairway, the light of the last forty minutes, the grass holding its colour a little longer than the sky does.

Hospitality / Volume I

A study of a café that remains open. KAAWA, Manchester, on a rainy evening in the part of the year when afternoons end early. Nine photographs, six chapters. The city goes dark. The room stays lit.

Publication 005

Against the Display

Garden / Volume I

A garden at Chelsea is never only a garden. This publication looks at two gardens that entered the show with their own atmosphere, memory and resistance intact — Sarah Price’s painterly, meadow-led sensibility and Jihae Hwang’s quieter, culturally rooted intelligence. Twenty-two photographs. Five sections. The tension between designed nature and institutional attention is the argument.

Publication 006

Morocco

Place / Volume I

A city at its own pace, and a palace that refuses to be anything less. Three studies across Tangier and Marrakech: the port city at street level and at dusk, its interiors and evening restaurants, and Dar El Bacha, where the ritual of coffee is placed inside something that was already beautiful before it arrived. Twenty-one photographs. Five sections.
