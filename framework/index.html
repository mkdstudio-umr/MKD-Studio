The Framework

A record of how the studio thinks.

The Framework is a document written before any commission begins. Seventeen pages describing how mkd STUDIO approaches photography, writing and design as a single integrated discipline, and why the output is always a publication rather than a collection of files.

The document covers the four subjects the studio works across: hospitality, places, craft and experience. For each, it describes not what the studio photographs but how it decides what matters. A hotel at the particular hour when the lobby is quiet. A city approached through the peripheral streets rather than the landmarks. A fragrance house photographed through the objects and spaces that give the work its character.

The Framework exists because commissioning an editorial publication requires a different conversation than booking a photographer. A client who has read it understands that the studio works toward a specific kind of result: one that documents what something actually feels like rather than how it has been positioned. The conversation that follows is shorter. The work that results is better.

That conversation begins on the Partnerships page. The studio behind it is described on the About page.

Not a price list. Not a service brochure. A record of how the studio actually works.
